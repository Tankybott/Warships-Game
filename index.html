General game rules

Each player has the same number of ships to place. When placing ships, it must be ensured that the distance from other ships is at least one grid square, including diagonally.

After placing the ships, the shooting phase begins, with the starting player selected randomly. After each missed shot, the turn order switches, while after each hit, the player who scored the hit can take another shot.

The player who sinks all of the opponent's ships wins.

Ship Placement:

To place a ship in a chosen location, click on the desired grid square.

If squares turn red, it indicates that the ship collides with other ships or the edge of the map, in which case another location must be chosen.

When the squares are green, it means the ship can be placed there.

To confirm the ship's position, press the "Place" button, then proceed to place the next ship or enter the shooting phase (if it was the last ship to be placed).

If you wish to change the position of a placed (but not yet confirmed) ship, click the "Change Place" button.

To change the direction in which the ship is placed, click the "Rotate" button.

Shooting:

The grid used for shooting is colored gold. To take a shot, click on the desired grid square, then press the "Shoot" button.

The locations where the player has fired shots are marked with a cross, while hit squares are marked with a cross inside a circle. You must pay attention to the feedback from the terminal.

When an opponent's ship is sunk, the terminal informs us of this. You must remember which ships are sunk and utilize the information that squares adjacent to a sunk ship cannot contain another ship!

Ships Display:

The screen displaying active ships shows how many ships we have remaining active (not sunk). When a ship is placed, its color changes from dimmed to brighter.

In the event of a ship being destroyed, it returns to a dimmed color.
